Official Contest Rules

These rules protect your work and keep the contest fair. Select any section to read it.

1. Contest Overview

Write an original short story explaining how Adam and Eve became the origin point of mankind without humanity arising through incest. The story must be told from the perspective of a witness who observed the event.

The witness may be human or nonhuman and must have a meaningful reason to be present and a distinct perspective.

2. Deadline

Entries must be received by October 31, 2026, at 11:59 p.m. Eastern Time. Late submissions will not be considered.

3. Prize

The winner receives one $200 USD Amazon gift card and may be invited to discuss paid future writing opportunities. Future work is not guaranteed and would require a separate agreement.

This contest is not sponsored, endorsed, administered by, or associated with Amazon.

4. Entry Fee

There is no entry fee. No purchase is necessary.

5. Eligibility

Open to writers age eighteen or older. International entries are welcome where lawful. If Amazon.com cannot reasonably be redeemed in the winner’s country, an equivalent regional Amazon gift card may be provided.

Employees, contractors, judges, and immediate family of anyone involved in judging are ineligible.

6. Word Count

Preferred length is 2,500 to 3,500 words. The absolute maximum is 4,000 words. Longer entries may be disqualified unread. Title and author information do not count.

7. Story Requirements

The entry must be original fiction, in English, previously unpublished, told from a witness’s perspective, explain the beginning of humanity without incest among Adam and Eve’s descendants, and be a complete story.

The solution may not rely upon:

  • Incest between Adam and Eve’s descendants
  • Aliens or extraterrestrial intervention
  • Modern or futuristic technology
  • Time travel or alternate universes
  • Cloning or scientific experimentation
  • A hidden civilization of ordinary humans already nearby

Biblical tradition, mythology, fantasy, speculative fiction, folklore, and original cosmology are welcome. Existing fictional canon is not required.

8. Originality & Publication

The story must be the submitting writer’s work and not publicly published. Private critique groups, workshops, beta readers, and private manuscript exchanges are allowed.

Simultaneous submissions are allowed, but the story must be withdrawn if accepted elsewhere before winner selection. Plagiarism, unauthorized adaptation, or another person’s work results in disqualification.

9. Generative AI Policy

The entrant must write the story. Generative AI may not draft or rewrite prose, generate scenes, passages, characters, dialogue, descriptions, or plot solutions, expand outlines into narrative, or substantially edit the manuscript.

Ordinary spelling correction, grammar checking, formatting help, and non-generative proofreading are permitted. Entrants may be asked to confirm compliance.

10. Number of Entries

One story per writer. Multiple versions or identities may disqualify all associated entries. Minor corrections may be accepted before the deadline; substantial replacements are at the organizer’s discretion.

13. Winner Notification

The winner will be contacted by email and must respond within fourteen calendar days. Failure to respond, verify eligibility, or comply may result in another winner being selected. The accepted winner’s preferred name and story title may be announced. No story will be posted without permission.

Entrants retain all copyright and ownership. Submission grants only a limited, nonexclusive right to receive, store, copy, and review the story for judging and administration.

No publication, adaptation, film, merchandising, or ownership rights are granted. Commercial use requires a separate written agreement. The contest premise, prompt, judging criteria, and independently created projects remain their creators’ property. Similar ideas do not automatically establish infringement; entries are judged on original expression.

15. Future Writing Opportunities

The organizer may contact the winner or other promising entrants about paid work. Winning does not guarantee employment, publication, representation, or a contract. Separate discussions create no obligation.

16. Disqualification

Grounds include a late entry, over 4,000 words, violating the premise or AI policy, plagiarism, another person’s work, false information, multiple entries, unreadable files, harassment, manipulation of judging, or unlawful conduct. Violations discovered later may still disqualify an entry.

17. Privacy

Personal information will be used only to administer the contest, verify eligibility, communicate when needed, and deliver the prize. Entrants are not added to a mailing list and their information will not be sold.

18. Changes or Cancellation

The organizer may modify, suspend, extend, or cancel due to fraud, technical failure, legal concerns, insufficient eligible entries, or circumstances preventing fair administration. Significant changes will be communicated through the contest’s promotional channels.
